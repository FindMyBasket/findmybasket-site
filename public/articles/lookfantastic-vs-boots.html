Beauty Bay vs Boots vs Escentual — Which is Cheaper for Skincare in the UK?

By FindMyBasket  ·  Updated April 2026  ·  5 min read

Boots, Beauty Bay and Escentual are three of the most popular destinations for skincare in the UK — but they are very different retailers with meaningfully different price points. Here is an honest guide to when each one wins.

The general picture

Across most popular skincare brands, online specialist retailers consistently price more competitively than Boots. The difference ranges from around 5% to 20% depending on the brand and product. On a single item the saving might seem modest, but across a full skincare routine bought quarterly it adds up to a meaningful amount per year.

That said, Boots fights back in areas where online retailers simply cannot compete — its physical store network, Advantage Card loyalty scheme and pharmacy services give it genuine advantages for certain shopping occasions.

What each retailer does best

💻 Online beauty retailers
Consistently more competitive pricing on popular skincare brands. Better for planned purchases where price matters more than immediacy.
🧪 Pharmacy specialists
Often strongest on dermatologist brands like La Roche-Posay, Avène and Bioderma. Good for European pharmacy skincare ranges.
🏪 Boots
Best for in-store convenience, same-day collection and Advantage Card loyalty. Worth it if you need something urgently or value points.

When Boots wins

  • You need something today and can collect in store
  • You are an Advantage Card collector who factors points into your spending
  • You want to try a product before committing to a full size
  • You are buying pharmacy-only items or need a consultation

When online retailers win

  • You are doing a planned shop and ordering multiple products
  • You want a wider range — online retailers often stock brands Boots does not carry
  • You are buying premium brands where the price difference is most significant
  • You can easily hit the free delivery threshold with your order

The smarter approach — compare across all retailers

The best price on your full routine is rarely at a single retailer. A four-product routine might have two products most competitively priced at one retailer and one at another — meaning the optimal basket splits. Factoring in delivery costs on top of product prices makes the calculation even more complex.
