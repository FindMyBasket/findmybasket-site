How Much Should a Full Skincare Routine Actually Cost in the UK?

By FindMyBasket  ·  Updated July 2026  ·  6 min read

A full skincare routine in the UK can cost anywhere from around £25 to well over £200, and the honest answer is that the range is enormous because it depends on how many steps you run, which brands you buy, and, crucially, where you buy them. A simple three-step routine of cleanser, moisturiser, and SPF sits at the lower end. A fuller routine with a serum, an exfoliant, an eye cream, and a treatment product climbs quickly. The part almost nobody talks about is that the same routine can cost different amounts depending on which UK retailers you buy each product from, and that gap is where most people quietly overspend.

This guide breaks down what a skincare routine actually costs by number of steps, where the money goes, and how to bring the total down without dropping the products you like.

What goes into a skincare routine, and where the cost sits

A skincare routine is built from a few core jobs: cleansing, treating, moisturising, and protecting. You do not need every step, and more steps do not automatically mean better skin. Here is roughly how cost stacks up as a routine grows.

A minimal routine is a cleanser, a moisturiser, and a daily SPF. This is genuinely all most people need, and it is the most cost-efficient setup because three products last a long time.

A standard routine adds a serum, usually a vitamin C for the morning or a hydrating or anti-ageing serum, plus perhaps a gentle exfoliant a few times a week. This is the most common shape of routine and where spend tends to settle for people who take skincare seriously without going overboard.

A full routine layers in an eye cream, a targeted treatment such as a retinoid or an acid, a separate night moisturiser, and sometimes a mask or an essence. This is where totals can run high, particularly if several products come from prestige brands.

The single biggest cost driver is brand tier. Budget-friendly ranges and the well-known affordable actives brands, including many of the K-beauty brands that have quietly reset expectations on value, can deliver a complete routine for a modest total. Mid-range and prestige brands can multiply that several times over for products that do broadly the same job. Neither is wrong. The point is knowing where your money is going so you choose on purpose rather than by accident.

Why two people with the same routine pay different amounts

Here is the part that surprises people. Two people can buy the exact same products and pay noticeably different totals, simply because of where they shop. Where a product is stocked by more than one UK retailer, the gap between the highest and lowest price averages around 25 percent, and for roughly one product in four that gap is 40 percent or more once you account for ongoing promotions, multibuy offers, and the natural variation between stockists. A serum that is full price at one retailer might be on a rotating offer at another. A moisturiser might be bundled in a gift-with-purchase somewhere else.

On a single product, a few pounds here or there feels minor. Across a full basket of five or six items, those gaps compound into a meaningful difference. This is the quiet maths of skincare spending, and it is almost never reflected in the typical best-products article, because those articles look at one product at a time and rarely at real, current UK pricing. If you want to see it for yourself, pick a product you already use and compare it across UK retailers before you reorder.

The delivery cost nobody adds up

There is a second hidden cost that throws off almost every estimate: delivery. If you buy your cleanser from one retailer, your serum from another because it was on offer, and your SPF from a third, you can easily end up paying three separate delivery charges. Each one is small, but together they can wipe out the saving you thought you were making by chasing the best price on each item.

This is why pricing a routine product by product is misleading. The number that actually matters is the total cost of the whole basket, delivered. Sometimes buying everything from one retailer that stocks all your products, even if one or two items are not at their individual best price, works out better value once delivery is included. Other times splitting across two retailers wins. You cannot know without comparing the whole basket, which is exactly the thing single-product guides never do.

A realistic way to budget for your routine

Rather than fixating on a single number, it helps to think in tiers and decide which one fits you.

If you want a complete, effective routine on a sensible budget, build around affordable actives brands, high-street skincare, and K-beauty, where the value is often strongest. A three to four step routine here is very achievable for a modest monthly outlay, because the products are inexpensive per unit and last for weeks.

If you want a mix of affordable basics and one or two hero products you love, a hybrid approach works well. Keep your cleanser and moisturiser budget-friendly, then spend on the serum or treatment that genuinely matters to you.

If you prefer prestige throughout, the routine will cost more, but the cross-retailer gaps tend to be widest in cash terms at this tier, which means comparing where you buy makes the biggest difference here of all.

Whichever tier you land on, the way to avoid overpaying is the same: decide on your products first, then build your routine and compare the whole basket across UK retailers with delivery included before you check out.
