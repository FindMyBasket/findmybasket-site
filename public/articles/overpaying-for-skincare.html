10 Skincare Products UK Shoppers Are Paying Too Much For in 2026

By FindMyBasket  ·  Updated April 2026  ·  5 min read

Here is something most UK skincare shoppers do not realise: the same product can cost meaningfully more depending on which retailer you use. Not because the quality differs, it is the identical item, but because retailers price independently. These are ten of the most popular products where the UK retailer price gap is biggest.

The 10 products

01

CeraVe Moisturising Cream 340g

One of the bestselling moisturisers in the UK and one of the most price-variable. The identical 340g tub can cost several pounds more at high street retailers versus online specialists. Given how regularly people restock it, that difference compounds over a year.

02

La Roche-Posay Toleriane Double Repair Moisturiser

La Roche-Posay products carry a premium at pharmacies and high street retailers. Online beauty retailers typically price them 10-15% more competitively, for the identical product with the same authenticity guarantee.

03

The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%

The most popular The Ordinary product in the UK. Despite being an affordable brand, the price still varies per bottle depending on retailer. Modest per unit but meaningful across a full The Ordinary routine.

04

Elemis Pro-Collagen Marine Cream 50ml

At the premium end of skincare, the stakes are higher. Elemis Pro-Collagen Marine Cream varies meaningfully across UK retailers, on a product at this price point, saving 10% is a significant amount in real terms.

05

Clarins Double Serum 50ml

Clarins products are priced at RRP by some retailers but below RRP by others. On an £89 product, even a 10% variation is a meaningful saving, and Clarins is a product people repurchase regularly.

06

CosRx AHA 7 Whitehead Power Liquid 100ml

One of the most popular K-beauty exfoliants now widely available in the UK. Prices vary across retailers as availability has grown, checking before you buy can save a few pounds each time.

07

CeraVe AM Facial Moisturising Lotion SPF50

Used every single morning, making it one of the highest-frequency repurchases in any skincare routine. Even a small price difference per bottle adds up significantly over a year of daily use.

08

Beauty of Joseon Rice and Prebiotic Sunscreen SPF50

One of the most talked-about K-beauty SPFs to reach UK retailers. As availability grows, so does price variation, worth comparing before every purchase.

09

The Ordinary AHA 30% + BHA 2% Peeling Solution 30ml

A cult chemical exfoliant used sparingly, once or twice per week, meaning each bottle lasts a long time. But prices still vary between retailers, and the difference compounds across multiple repurchases.

10

Clinique Moisture Surge 72H Auto-Replenishing Hydrator

A beloved hydrating moisturiser widely stocked across UK retailers. The difference between the highest and lowest UK price on the 75ml can be several pounds, worth a quick comparison before buying.

How much could you actually save?

If you buy four to five products consistently from the highest-priced retailer versus the best available price, you could easily spend £15-25 more per shop than necessary. Across a year with quarterly restocks, that is £60-100 in unnecessary spend on the exact same products.
