How to build a skincare routine for under £40

FindMyBasket  ·  5 min read

Good skincare does not have to cost a fortune. With the right products and the right retailers, you can build a genuinely effective daily skincare routine for under £40 and keep it topped up without breaking the bank.

What does a basic routine need?

Before spending anything, it helps to understand what a basic routine actually requires. For most people, the essentials are a cleanser to remove dirt and oil without stripping the skin, a moisturiser to hydrate and protect the skin barrier, and an SPF which is the single most important anti-ageing step you can take.

Everything else - serums, toners, eye creams, exfoliants - is optional. Get the basics right first.

How to keep costs down

Buy bigger sizes

Cleansers and moisturisers are used daily, so bigger sizes work out much cheaper per ml. CeraVe's 340g moisturising cream lasts months and costs less per use than a smaller tub.

Shop online rather than in store

High street prices are almost always higher than online. LOOKFANTASTIC, Cult Beauty and Beauty Bay consistently undercut Boots on popular skincare brands.

Hit the free delivery threshold

Most online beauty retailers offer free delivery over £25. With a routine of even three products you will usually get there, so there is no reason to pay for delivery.

Compare before you buy

Prices vary significantly between retailers for the same products. A quick comparison before checkout can save you several pounds per order and those savings add up over a year.

What to add when you are ready to spend more

  • A vitamin C serum in the morning - brightens skin and boosts SPF protection
  • A niacinamide serum - reduces pores, controls oil and evens skin tone
  • A retinol at night - the gold standard for anti-ageing, start slowly
  • An exfoliant once or twice a week - removes dead skin cells for a brighter complexion
