K-Beauty in the UK — Where to Find the Best Prices in 2026

By FindMyBasket  ·  Updated April 2026  ·  6 min read

Korean skincare has gone from niche to mainstream in the UK in the space of a few years. Boots reported in early 2026 that K-beauty sales had grown fivefold, with a Korean skincare product selling every 11 seconds. CosRx, Beauty of Joseon, ANUA and Mixsoon are now household names for British skincare enthusiasts.

But while K-beauty is widely available, prices for the same product can vary dramatically between UK retailers. We tracked over 800 K-beauty products across major UK skincare sites — and the gaps are bigger than most shoppers realise.

Where is K-beauty cheapest in the UK?

No single retailer wins across every K-beauty brand. Different UK sites have different brand specialisms, pricing structures and discount schedules. From our tracking of over 800 K-beauty products, here is what we found:

Specialist K-beauty retailers

Sites like Skin Cupid have the deepest K-beauty range in the UK, stocking brands that are hard to find elsewhere — Mixsoon, Numbuzin, Haruharu Wonder, Dr Ceuracle and Biodance among them. Prices are competitive on hero products but vary across the range.

Branded Beauty consistently offers the lowest prices on the most popular K-beauty products like CosRx and Beauty of Joseon, often £3-10 below specialist competitors. The trade-off is a smaller K-beauty range.

Mass-market retailers

Boots and Superdrug now stock a meaningful K-beauty range and frequently run promotions — particularly Boots' 3-for-2 mix-and-match deals across skincare. Prices are often higher than specialist sites but loyalty rewards and deals can close the gap during sale periods.

Amazon offers the broadest range overall, but prices vary widely between Amazon-fulfilled and third-party sellers. Always check the seller before purchasing — counterfeit K-beauty products are a known issue on Amazon marketplace listings.

How to save money on K-beauty in the UK

  • Compare before you buy — the same product can vary by 30-46% across UK retailers. Tools like FindMyBasket let you check live prices in seconds.
  • Buy your full routine in one place where possible — splitting across retailers means paying delivery twice, which can wipe out the savings.
  • Free delivery thresholds matter — most K-beauty retailers offer free delivery from £25-35. Adding one extra product to hit the threshold often costs less than paying for delivery.
  • Watch for retailer-specific deals — Skin Cupid runs frequent loyalty offers, Boots has 3-for-2, Cult Beauty does themed sales. Timing your purchase around these can save another 10-30%.
  • Avoid suspiciously cheap Amazon sellers — counterfeit K-beauty is a known problem. Stick to Amazon-fulfilled or trusted UK skincare retailers.
  • Skip the import sites — buying directly from Korea or Yesstyle used to save money but UK retailers have largely closed the gap, and customs fees often eliminate any saving.
